What is TEP?

The Tutorial Education Program offers flexible, paid roles for students who want to build their CV, strengthen academic skills, and make a campus-wide impact.

Structured mentorship and training with experienced staff.

Opportunities to connect coursework with real-world projects.

Support for both English and Swedish speaking students.

Our Three Pillars

Every participant blends core responsibilities across teaching, research, and community work. You can focus on one track or rotate between them based on interest and availability.

Teaching Assistance

Guide lab sessions, answer course questions, and help classmates decode complex concepts.

  • Flexible mentorship hours that fit around your studies.
  • Real classroom experience with feedback from instructors.

Research Support

Work alongside faculty on active projects—from data analysis to prototyping and documentation.

  • Hands-on practice using academic tools and methods.
  • Co-create results you can proudly showcase in your CV.

Community Outreach

Design events and workshops that invite new voices into computing and celebrate diversity.

  • Collaborate with schools, associations, and local partners.
  • Inspire future students through interactive activities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can join the Tutorial Education Program?

Undergraduate students from their second year onward and master’s students enrolled in Computer Science or Media Technology at LNU.

Do I need previous teaching experience?

No. We provide mentoring, onboarding workshops, and resources to help you feel confident supporting your peers.

How much time do I have to commit?

You can work part-time at 25% or 50% depending on course load and availability. We schedule activities to fit your studies.

What types of projects will I work on?

Assignments range from lab tutoring and research support to community workshops, coding clubs, and outreach events.
